# Fitness Criteria: Standup Comedy Bit

## Goal
Evolve the funniest possible standup comedy bit about AI and technology. Should work as a 30-60 second chunk of a set — not a one-liner, not a full 5-minute bit.

## Constraints
- Must be about AI, coding, or tech culture
- 3-8 sentences long (a tight bit, not a ramble)
- Must have clear setup → punchline structure (can have multiple punchlines)
- Written as spoken word — contractions, casual language, pauses marked with "..."
- No offensive humor (no punching down on specific groups)
- Must be ORIGINAL — no recycled "robots taking over" cliches

## What "better" means (in priority order)
1. **Punchline lands** — the laugh moment must be unexpected. Misdirection > predictable
2. **Relatability** — audience should think "oh my god, YES" because they've lived it
3. **Specificity** — specific details are funnier than vague ones ("Gmail's undo send" > "email")
4. **Rhythm** — good comedy has musical timing. Short setup, punchy delivery, breathing room
5. **Callback potential** — bonus if an early detail pays off later in the bit
6. **Act-out quality** — would this be funny performed on stage, not just read?

## Scoring Guide
- 0-2: not funny, reads like a blog post or a dad joke
- 3-4: has a joke structure but the punchline is predictable or the observation is stale
- 5-6: gets a chuckle, decent premise, but needs tighter writing
- 7-8: genuinely funny, good misdirection, would get laughs from a live audience
- 9-10: killer bit. Would work in a Netflix special. Multiple laugh moments, tight writing, surprising angle, memorable tag line
